Overview

The hostels in the JGI campus have a manual out-pass system with the issue of a paper out-pass sheet, which is a time-consuming process for students and wardens. To improve this system we developed this Hostel Management System in our Android and IOS development course lab.

The Existing Out-Pass filling Sheet

Approach

With the Hostel Management System in place, we wanted to help students check their out-pass status online and improve security and verification near the campus exits. And so, we started our development process in three stages.

Stage 1 - Research

What are the different user problems with the existing system ?

Through our research and surveys with the users, we gathered information on the different user needs and analyzed the information to identify user needs and develop design ideas.

Stage 2 - Design

How can we meet the user needs with design solutions?

Our research findings were translated into design ideas that centered on a secure verification method and created an information architecture.

Stage 3 - Deployment & Evaluation

Deployment

When we presented the project to our administration, a web version of the application was requested with their needs.


Accordingly we developed a web version using JavaScript, HTML, CSS and Bootstrap for the frontend and Python, Flask and JSON for backend services and integrated web view into the android application.


This application went live from January 2019 in www.sbmjcehostel.com but due to the lockdown in India the JGI hostels temporarily closed their hostels and the website is temporarily shut from late 2021.


Results

We got a positive response from the students, wardens and security after the deployment as it saved time and met their needs.


We were able to limit the studentsâ€™ visits to the wardenâ€™s offices to apply and check their out-pass status.


The wardens were able to accept or reject the out-pass from the comfort of their hostel offices.


Wardens had the student records handy in case of an emergency or if a management cross-verification was to happen.


The security no longer had to call the warden to verify if the date and out-pass numbers were correct and if the student had tampered the paper out-pass sheet.
